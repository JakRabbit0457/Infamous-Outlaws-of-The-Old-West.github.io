Notable Outlaw Gangs:

On this page is a list of notable outlaw gangs from the Old West of which are mentioned in the seperate outlaw pages on this website. Each listed outlaw gang has a summary which includes:
  • When/where the gang was active in the Old West
  • The members of the gang
  • what the gang is most infamous for

     

    James-Younger Gang

    The James-Younger Gang was active primarily in Missouri from 1866 to 1876. The gang was composed of the Younger brothers Cole, Bob, Jim, and John Younger, brothers Jesse and Frank James, and other outlaws such as Arthur C. McCoy, Clell Miller, Charlie Pitts, and Bill Chadwell. The gang successfully robbed several stagecoaches, trains, and banks in Missouri including banks in Richmond, Lexington, and Gallatin, Missouri. They are most infamous for their attempted and unsuccessful robbery of the Northfield bank in Northfield, Minnesota on September 7th, 1876 which led to the death of two members of the gang and sparked a manhunt which would lead to the eventual capture of the Younger brothers.

     

    Dalton Gang

    The Dalton Gang was active primarily in Kansas and Indian Territory/Oklahoma Territory from 1890 to 1892. The gang was comprised of brothers Bob, Emmett, Bill, and Grattan or “Grat” Dalton, and included other outlaws over the years such as Charley Pierce, George “Bitter Creek” Newcomb, Richard “Dick” Broadwell, Charlie Bryant, Bill Doolin, and Bill Power. The gang was known for robbing trains and banks. They are most infamous for their ambitious and overall brutal end in Coffeyville, Kansas where on October 5th, 1892 they attempted to simultaneously rob two banks (the First National Bank and the C.M. Condon & Company’s Bank) in broad daylight. On the day of the robbery a final shootout against lawmen and the armed townspeople of Coffeyville would lead to the deaths of multiple of the gang members including Bob and Grat Dalton, Bill Power, and Dick Broadwell. Emmett Dalton was the only survivor. This would be the end of the Dalton Gang.

     

    The Wild Bunch/Doolin-Dalton Gang

    “The Wild Bunch” or the Doolin-Dalton Gang, was active in primarily Kansas and Indian Territory/Oklahoma Territory from 1892 to 1896. The gang included Bill Doolin (the gang’s leader), Bill Dalton, George “Bitter Creek” Newcomb, Charley Pierce, William “Tulsa Jack” Blake, Oliver Yantis, Dan "Dynamite Dick" Clifton, Richard “Little Dick” West, George "Red Buck" Waightman, Roy Daugherty or “Arkansas Tom Jones”, and “Little Bill” Raidler along with others. The gang robbed trains and banks in Kansas, Missouri, and Indian Territory (present day Oklahoma) and are most infamous for their legendary confrontation and shootout with multiple Deputy U.S. Marshals in The Battle of Ingalls on September 1st, 1893.
